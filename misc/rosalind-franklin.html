The jewish scientist that deserves credit

Rosalind Franklin was a famous scientist who captured Photo 51, which proved that DNA has a double helical structure, although she was not credided for it. She was born in London on July 25, 1920, and was interested in science from a young age.

She was sent to a boarding school at nine in 1929, then to one of the only schools that taught science to girls, St. Pauls Girls School, when she was eleven in 1931. When she graduated high school, she got a full scholarship for Cambridge University in 1938. At Cambridge, she examined different types of coals and carbon to see which ones were safe to use in gas masks for soldiers of World War II, which started in 1939. Her research helped the British make safer gas masks.

Rosalind graduated the university in 1941 and the war ended in 1945. She then moved to Paris and got a job as a research chemist in a goverment lab for three years, from 1947 to 1950. After that, Rosalind went back to London to work at a lab at Kings College. At Kings, she and Maurice Wilkins studied studied the structure of DNA.

After two years, in 1952, Rosalind took Photo 51. This photo showed a helix made up of repeating strands of DNA.
